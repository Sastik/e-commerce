1. What is trade cycle?

A trade cycle is the series of exchanges that occur when a commercial exchange is executed between a customer and a supplier.

Types of Trade Cycle:

  1. Economic Cycle: The overall pattern of growth and contraction in an economy, typically lasting 5-10 years.
  2. Business Cycle: Fluctuations in business activity during different phases of the economic cycle, consisting of four stages: recovery, prosperity, recession, and depression.
  3. Financial Cycle: Shifts in financial markets, such as stock prices, bond yields, or foreign exchange rates.

Phases of a Trade Cycle:

  • Depression: Economic activity is very low. Income, production, employment, prices, and profits fall. Resources are idle, and pessimism prevails, leading to business closures.
  • Recovery: The economy starts to improve. Output, employment, income, and prices rise slowly. Demand and investment increase, and optimism returns, leading to further growth.
  • Prosperity: Economic conditions are strong. There is high income and employment, no idle resources, rising wages, prices, profits, and interest rates.
  • Recession: Businesses cut back on investment, employment, and production due to realized mistakes. This leads to falling income, expenditure, prices, and profits.

3. What is e-commerce?

E-commerce refers to performing online commercial activities and transactions over the internet. It includes activities such as buying and selling products, making monetary transactions, etc., over the internet. The internet is used for e-commerce.

Examples of e-commerce include online retailers like Flipkart, Amazon, and Paytm Mall.

Different Types of E-commerce Platforms:

  • Business-to-Business (B2B): This is when a transaction of goods or services occurs between two businesses.
    • Examples include online marketplaces, procurement portals, and wholesale platforms.
  • Business-to-Consumer (B2C): In B2C e-commerce, businesses sell products or services directly to individual consumers.
    • Examples include online retail stores, such as Amazon, Walmart.com, and eBay.
  • Consumer-to-Business (C2B): C2B e-commerce involves consumers selling goods and services to companies.
    • For example, an individual selling an image to a newspaper is conducting a C2B transaction.
  • Consumer-to-Consumer (C2C): C2C describes business transactions involving two or more consumers.
    • Example: When a person sells their car to another person, thatâ€™s a C2C transaction.
  • Business-To-Administration (B2A): This e-commerce category refers to all transactions between companies and public administration.
  • Consumer-To-Administration (C2A): C2A e-commerce encompasses all electronic transactions between individuals and public administration.

Risks in E-commerce:

  • Refunds and Client Disputes: Refunds and client disputes have become a common risk for all e-commerce stores due to issues such as wrong product delivery, defective products, or a customer having been charged for the same product twice.
  • Stolen Credit Card Information: Credit card information stolen from websites can lead to unauthenticated transactions, costing customers money.
  • Poor Customer Service: Customer satisfaction is the backbone of any e-commerce business. If customers are not satisfied, the chances of repeat purchases are very low.

  • Harassment and Stalking: Cyber lawyers both prosecute and defend people when stalking occurs using the internet and other forms of electronic communication.

Advantages of Cyber Law:

8. Write a short note on a) Mobile computing b) WAP

a) Mobile Computing

Mobile computing is the way to transmit data, voice, and video via a computer or any other wireless-enabled device without being connected to a fixed physical link.

In this technology, data transmission is done wirelessly with the help of wireless devices such as mobiles, laptops, etc.

Types of Mobile Computing:

  • Mobile infrastructure: Includes the wireless networks, wireless protocols, and data formats.
  • Mobile hardware: Consists of mobile devices or device components that can be used to receive or access the service of mobility.
  • Mobile Software: A program that runs on mobile hardware.

Advantages:

  • Portability: Mobile devices are smaller and more portable than traditional computers.
  • Affordability: Over time, mobile devices have become less expensive and easier to obtain.
  • Wireless communications: You can communicate from any remote location without being physically present there.

Disadvantages:

  • Battery issue: Short battery life, limited processing power, smaller screen size.
  • Data security: Mobile computing raises significant data security vulnerabilities.

b) WAP

The Wireless Application Protocol (WAP) is a set of communication protocols and an application programming model based on the World Wide Web (WWW).

The WAP model consists of 3 levels known as Client, Gateway, and Origin Server.

Working Process:

  1. When a user opens the browser in their mobile device and selects a website that they want to view, the mobile device sends the URL encoded request via a network to a WAP gateway using WAP protocol.
  2. The request sent via mobile to WAP gateway is called an encoding request.
  3. The sent encoding request is translated through WAP gateway and then forwarded in the form of a conventional HTTP URL request over the Internet.

Advantages:

  • WAP can be used over multiple platforms.
  • Higher controlling possibilities are offered.
  • You can send and receive real-time data with WAP.

Disadvantages:

  • WAP connection speed is slow and the number of connections is less.
  • Less secure.

13. What are the SCP tools?

Supply Chain Planning (SCP) involves strategies for balancing the supply and demand of goods and services. Various software tools assist in these processes.

  • Inventory Planning: Optimizes inventory levels to meet demand while minimizing costs.
  • Production Planning and Scheduling: Plans and schedules manufacturing activities efficiently.
  • Distribution Planning: Manages the distribution of goods to various locations.
  • Strategic Network Design: Designs the overall supply chain network for optimal performance.
  • Inventory Strategy Optimization: Develops strategies to optimize inventory across the supply chain.
  • Supply Planning: Coordinates the supply of raw materials and components.

24. What is firewall in ecommerce? Define data encryption?

In e-commerce, a firewall is a security measure that acts as a barrier between a trusted internal network and untrusted external networks, like the internet.

Key Points about Firewalls in E-commerce:

  • Security Barrier: Monitors and controls network traffic to protect the e-commerce platform.
  • Traffic Control: Regulates incoming and outgoing network traffic based on predefined security rules.
  • Prevents Unauthorized Access: Blocks unauthorized attempts and malicious activities from external sources.
  • Access Regulation: Controls which applications and services can communicate with the e-commerce platform, enhancing security.

Firewalls are critical for safeguarding e-commerce platforms and ensuring the security of customer data and transactions.

Data Encryption

Data encryption is the process of transforming data into a secret code (ciphertext) to ensure it remains confidential and secure.

Key Points about Data Encryption:

  • Confidentiality: Encrypting data protects its confidentiality by making it unreadable to unauthorized users.
  • Ciphertext: Encrypted data is known as ciphertext, which can only be decrypted with the correct decryption key or password.
  • Importance: Essential for preserving data integrity and confidentiality, preventing unauthorized access and data breaches.

Data encryption serves as a critical security measure in protecting sensitive information in e-commerce and other digital transactions.

25. Define Digital Signatures and Digital Certificates

Digital Certificate

A digital certificate is a certificate issued by a Certificate Authority (CA) to verify the identity of the certificate holder.

Advantages of Digital Certificate:

  • Verification: Facilitates cybersecurity by restricting access to sensitive data, ensuring authentication.
  • Identification: Contains information about the certificate holder.
  • Trust Establishment: Issued by trusted Certificate Authorities (CAs) after validating the identity of the certificate holder.

Disadvantages of Digital Certificate:

  • Phishing Attacks: Attackers can create fake websites and obtain certificates to make their websites appear legitimate.
  • Weak Encryption: Older systems may use less secure encryption methods vulnerable to intrusions.
  • Misconfiguration: Incorrectly configured certificates can lead to security vulnerabilities in websites and online interactions.

Digital Signature

A digital signature is a mathematical technique used to validate the authenticity and integrity of a message, software, or digital document.

Benefits of Digital Signatures:

  • Legal Validity: Legally binding and ideal for any document requiring authentication.
  • Data Integrity: Ensures that transmitted data, including health information, remains unaltered between consenting parties.
  • Non-repudiation: Provides non-repudiation, meaning the sender cannot deny having signed the document.

Drawbacks of Digital Signature:

  • Dependency on Technology: Susceptible to cybercrimes like hacking due to technology dependence.
  • Complexity: Setting up and using digital signatures can be challenging for some users.
  • Limited Acceptance: Takes time to replace traditional signatures due to technology adoption challenges.

28. Define Quality Management.

Quality management involves overseeing all activities and tasks to maintain a high level of excellence. It ensures that an organization, product, or service consistently performs well.

Main Components of Quality Management:

  • Quality Planning: Determining quality standards and strategies to achieve them.
  • Quality Assurance: Ensuring processes are in place to meet quality standards.
  • Quality Control: Monitoring and testing products or services to ensure they meet quality criteria.
  • Quality Improvement: Continuously enhancing processes and products to improve quality.

Key Points:

  • Quality Policy: Establishing a policy that defines the organization's commitment to quality.
  • Implementation: Creating and executing plans to achieve and maintain quality standards.
  • TQM: Total Quality Management, focusing on continuous improvement and customer satisfaction.
  • Process Management: Managing quality across all stages, from initiation to delivery and beyond.

Quality management ensures that products and services consistently meet high standards throughout production and delivery.
